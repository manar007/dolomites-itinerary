🏔️ Your Dolomites Campervan Adventure

April 30 - May 4 | Milan to the Mountains & Back

⚠️ Important Spring Travel Advisory

Your trip is during the off-season (late April/early May). Cable cars will be CLOSED, high-altitude roads may be closed, and many trails will have snow. This itinerary focuses on what is realistically accessible: scenic drives, lake circuits, and lower-elevation walks. Always check local conditions before starting activities.

📅 Day 1: Milan → Ansitz Gamp, Klausen (Tuesday, April 30th)

A direct drive into the heart of South Tyrol to one of the most atmospheric campsites in the Dolomites. Ansitz Gamp sits in the picturesque Isarco Valley at Klausen — a medieval hilltop town with a dramatic castle perched above. Open year-round, only 2h 45min from Milan, and the perfect first taste of authentic South Tyrol.

🏕️ Your Campsite Base Tonight

✅ Ansitz Gamp Camper Stop, Klausen (Chiusa) — South Tyrol

Open: Year-round ✅ — accessible in all weather conditions

Arrival: Around 3:15 PM

Why here? Dramatic South Tyrolean scenery — a medieval castle looms directly above the campsite on a rocky cliff. Set in the Isarco Valley surrounded by mountains. Klausen is one of Italy's most beautiful historic towns, just a short walk away. Perfect first night with real Dolomite atmosphere.

Campervan Facilities: Electricity (6A), fresh water & sewage, SAT-TV, free WiFi, disposal station. Motorhomes up to 10m. Year-round access.

Note: The Camper Stop has no sanitary block. If you need showers/toilets, book a comfort pitch at the main campground instead (same location).

View on Google Maps Official Website
🏙️ Afternoon in Klausen
Option A — Explore Klausen old town: Walk into the medieval centre of Klausen (Chiusa) — one of Italy's most beautiful historic towns. Narrow lanes, frescoed facades, and the dramatic Sabiona Monastery perched on the cliff above.
Option B — Hike to Sabiona Monastery: An easy 45-minute walk up from Klausen to the Sabiona Monastery on the cliff. Breathtaking views over the Isarco Valley and surrounding mountains. Easy trail, suitable for all fitness levels.
Option C — Just settle in: It's the first day! Park up, enjoy a coffee looking at the castle above, and take a short stroll. Save your energy for the big days ahead.
🍽️ Where to Eat Tonight
Restaurant at Ansitz Gamp ⭐ On-site
The campsite's own restaurant serving South Tyrolean specialties. Convenient for a first evening after a long drive.
Gasthof Grauer Bär ⭐ 4.4/5 — Local favourite
Traditional South Tyrolean tavern in the heart of Klausen. Excellent Knödel, Speck platters, and local wine. 5-minute walk from campsite.
Pizzeria in Klausen ⭐ Casual & easy
A simple, relaxed pizza dinner in the old town. Perfect if you're tired from the drive and just want something easy and delicious.

Drive from Ansitz Gamp in Klausen directly into the heart of the Dolomites — Val Gardena. This is your first full day in the mountains. It's a short 1-hour drive, so you'll have the whole day to explore the iconic Dolomite landscapes around you.

🏞️ Day Activities — Choose Based on Energy & Weather

🏕️ Your Campsite Base Tonight — 3 Options
⚠️ May 1st is Italian Labour Day (Festa dei Lavoratori) — a national public holiday. Campsites in this area fill up fast. Book your preferred option well in advance!

🥇 Option 1 — Best Views: Camping Seiser Alm, Castelrotto

OPENS MAY 1ST ✅

Why here? The most spectacular campsite in the area — set in the Schlern-Rosengarten Nature Park with breathtaking direct views of the Sciliar/Schlern massif. Every evening you get the famous Dolomite 'Enrosadira' red glow from your pitch. Saltwater pool, shop, and restaurant on site.

Campervan: Comfort pitches with electricity (16A), water/wastewater, TV, gas, free WiFi. Private bathrooms available as add-on.

View on Google Maps Official Website

🥈 Option 2 — Wild & Remote: Camping Colfosco, Alta Badia

OPEN YEAR-ROUND ✅ — NO RESERVATION NEEDED

Why here? A wild, unspoiled campsite in the heart of Alta Badia at 1645m — surrounded by the most dramatic Dolomite rock walls on all sides. No advance booking needed, making it your perfect backup if Option 1 is full. The views of the Sella Group and Sassongher are extraordinary.

Campervan: Pitches for motorhomes and caravans. Basic but functional facilities. Dogs welcome. First come, first served — arrive by early afternoon.

Drive from Klausen: ~1h 20min via Bolzano and the Badia valley

View on Google Maps Official Website

🥉 Option 3 — Well-Equipped: Camping Alpe di Siusi, Siusi allo Sciliar

OPENS MAY 1ST ✅

Why here? Well-organised campsite with stunning views of the Sciliar massif. Perfect base for Alpe di Siusi, Seceda, and Val di Funes. Good facilities and restaurant on site.

Campervan: Modern facilities, restaurant, direct mountain views of the Dolomites.

📅 Day 3: Sunrise & Cortina Superstars (Thursday, May 2nd)

An early start to catch the magical sunrise at Lago di Braies, followed by exploring the legendary peaks and passes around Cortina d'Ampezzo.
